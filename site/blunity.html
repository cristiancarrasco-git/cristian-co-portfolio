01 · The problem

Water inequality is a human crisis

2.1B
people still lack access to clean drinking water
2B+
live in water-stressed countries (2021)
60%
of irrigated agriculture faces extreme water stress

Climate change compounds everything: floods push sewage into purification systems, droughts starve communities of supply. The insight that shaped the brand: water is a global issue, but the solutions can be local — the gap isn't awareness, it's connection.

02 · The intention

Five callings, one question

How can we connect culture and activism within humanity to improve water conditions for those who need it?

Group calling intention — refined with our professor

Culture is the doorway; activism is the path. Our research anchored the brand in water's cultural weight — Hindu rivers as deities, Vietnamese water puppetry and creation myths, Meso-American water as sacred agriculture — alongside the sharpest access crises those same communities face.

03 · Finding the name

Fourteen names, hundreds of marks

Water ReflectsAquarityHydrify The Blue CollectiveBluePulseAquanect AqualumeOshunHenosis Blunity ✓

Blue + unity — the mission is the name: people, gathered around water. Every member sketched marks by hand before anything went digital, hunting for the moment the two words read as one shape. The winning wordmark lets a ripple form the “un.”

05 · The website

From landing to action in one scroll

Four doors, one nav — About Us, Get Involved, Contact Us, Donate — with the ask always one click away. The mission leads: “access to clean water is a basic human right.”

06 · The ripple story

How one gallery becomes clean water

Gallery → Connecting → Activists → Grassroots organizations → Life. The gallery bridges culture and water through stories; shared experience turns visitors into participants; awareness becomes advocacy; Blunity routes that energy to organizations building long-term local water solutions — and better water means stronger communities and opportunity for the next generation.
